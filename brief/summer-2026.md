## 1. The Silk Road Table, read before it is eaten

Weeks 7-8: re-map the real corridors (Bustleton Ave, Forest Hills) and design the winter's eating as fieldwork, not ranking. Weeks 9-10: write the essay the Log was always reaching for — 'what my people ate at 2 AM and why' — and let the archive stand as an inheritance.

**First moves (week one).**

- Pull every histories-in-one-word thread and start a Thymer collection: 'lagman — three histories,' one card per tradition (Uyghur / Bukharian / Soviet-Russian)
- Read one serious piece on Bukharian Jewish foodways in Queens and one on Uyghur diaspora cooking; highlight into Readwise
- Re-drive nothing — instead map the Bustleton Ave and Rego Park corridors on paper, marking which restaurant belongs to which history
- Write 500 words answering his own line: 'I hadn't discovered a soup. I'd discovered an axis.' What is the axis?

## 5. The four-mode cosmos, deepened at the source

**Why now.** His Greco-Aegean world is fully realized as surface — four modes, four bespoke faces, meanders and metallic sheen — but built largely from amphora references rather than the deep well of classical material culture. He lives on the seam of 'the classical-antique surface over the Central-Asian subject.' A summer at the source would let the world-building rest on real vase-painting, real letterforms, real taxonomy — sincerity earning its rigor.

**The arc.** Weeks 1-2: Greek black-figure and Attic vase painting — the grammar behind Etruscan Mode. Weeks 3-4: the meander and Aegean ornament as a system, not a motif. Weeks 5-6: type and letterform history feeding his four faces (Aegean, Spartacus, Xiphos, Columns). Weeks 7-8: invented notation and constructed scripts — the tradition his molecular sigils belong to. Weeks 9-10: revise the four modes with what he's learned and design one new artifact — a specimen sheet or a notation grammar — that could only exist after this summer.

**First moves (week one).**

- Use the Whitney MCP to pull American works that touch antiquity, letterform, or taxonomy and start a reference board
- Read one primer on Greek black-figure technique; note what his Etruscan Mode gets right and what it invents
- Lay his four display faces beside real classical letterforms and mark the choices he'd now make differently
